Altar Mayor

The Altar Mayor is a natural rock formation about 10–15 meters high, partially submerged in the river and covered with green moss. It is surrounded by small stones used for scrubbing, madre cacao trees, bamboo, guava, and wild grasses, all of which create a natural and peaceful scenery.

Location: Altar Mayor

Amao Falls

Amao Falls is an eight-tiered waterfall located in a secluded part of Sitio Amao, Brgy. Silangang Malicboy, where clear water cascades down steep rocks. The area is surrounded by lush trees and other plants such as Tibig, Balete, and coconut, providing a calm view and fresh air for visitors.

Location: Amao Falls

Balete Beach

Balete Beach is a serene spot known for its fine sand and the presence of large Balete trees along the shoreline. The area is ideal for picnics, swimming, and relaxation, offering visitors a peaceful escape surrounded by nature. The shade from the Balete trees provides a cool and refreshing atmosphere, making it a favorite destination for families and groups of friends.

Location: Balete Beach

Balugbog Baboy Sandbar

The Balugbog Baboy Sandbar has long been known by local fishermen as a safe resting place during bad weather at sea. Its name comes from the Tagalog words "balugbog" (spine) and "baboy" (pig), because it resembles a pig's back when viewed from above. Over time, it has become a popular destination for tourists, vloggers, and personalities due to its natural beauty, and is also used for photoshoots, prenup sessions, and as a place for fishermen to exchange their catch.

Location: Balugbog Baboy Sandbar

Bilaran Sandbar

Bilaran Sandbar is a long stretch of white sand that emerges during low tide, creating a beautiful natural walkway surrounded by clear blue waters. It is a popular spot for beachgoers, offering a unique experience of walking on sand in the middle of the sea. The sandbar is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and taking scenic photos, especially during sunrise and sunset.

Location: Bilaran Sandbar

Daungang Pari

The original physical form of Daungan Pari was a natural muddy shoreline connected to the river and sea, home to various marine life and mangrove trees. Over time, interventions such as the construction of a concrete chapel and cross on the shore, as well as the arrival of informal settlers, have brought some changes to the cleanliness and natural state of the area. Although the main land and sea features remain, human activities like improper waste disposal and the building of structures have affected the environment.

Location: Daungang Pari

Iringan Falls

Iringan Falls is a magnificent waterfall with clean and cool water cascading from the top of sedimentary rocks, surrounded by lush forest and steep trails, providing a relaxing view and a natural ambiance.

Location: Iringan Falls

Kamaluya Falls

Kamaluya Falls is considered one of the tallest waterfalls in Pagbilao, Quezon, and is a popular destination for tourists, especially during the summer. According to locals, the trail leading to the falls was also used in the 1970s as a route by Japanese soldiers, giving the place historical significance. The falls have been part of environmental conservation projects, such as tree-planting initiatives led by the DENR, to protect nature and preserve the beauty of natural resources. It is also an important source of water and livelihood for the residents of Barangay Ilaya Bagumbungan.

Location: Kamaluya Falls

Kwebang Lampas

Kwebang Lampas is a natural cave with limestone formations along the edge of a white sand beach, surrounded by the sea, trees, and simple facilities. However, some areas around it have trash and cleanliness issues.

Location: Kwebang Lampas

La-Isla Salamin Cave

La Isla Salamin Cave is a natural cave located in a quiet part of an island in Pagbilao, Quezon. Its exterior is mostly made up of limestone rocks with natural shapes and holes formed by water and wind over time. Inside the cave, you can see natural stalactites and stalagmites, while the surroundings are bordered by clear sea, white sand on the shore, and lush trees that provide cool and fresh air. The scenery offers tranquility and the beauty of nature, perfect for those seeking peace and a nature getaway.

Location: La-Isla Salamin Cave

La-Isla Salamin

La Isla Salamin is an island with an area of 2.62 hectares, featuring a white sandy shoreline, clear seawater, and two mountains that appear as reflections of each other. Here you will find heart-shaped mangrove trees, two caves on opposite sides—one with a stone staircase—and plants such as coconut and yucca. The island is surrounded by vibrant marine life, has a riprap protecting the shore, and offers views of the ocean and nearby lands, giving the place natural beauty and historical significance

Location: La-Isla Salamin

Lima-aw

The Lima-aw mountain was located at the center of Pagbilao Grande Island. This land formation is a group or chain of mountains close together with ranges of similar form, size and age. It can be compared to a neighborhood of houses all built around the same time. While they are not identical, they share similar features and in their overall square footage. The Lima-aw mountain ranges have steep, sloping sides and sharp and rounded ridges. Most of the summit part of the mountain is pointed top but there is one of the summits that has a flat surface which can be found at the center area of the mountain ranges.

Location: Lima-aw

Mangilalan Cave

Mangilalan is one of the most recognized caves not only in Pagbilao but the whole province of Quezon. It became famous after being documented and televised several times. Hidden beneath the high forests of Brgy. Ilayang Bagumbungan, it is found covered with thick brownish-green moss along with a plant that is similar to the famous plant “monstera” that locals called “sahod-yaman” or “lukmoy” eppipanium pinnatum. Other unknown types of plants are found surrounding most of the above surface of the cave. The mouth of the cave can fit not more than three people entering at the same time. A small amount of water from the waterfall inside .

Location: Mangilalan Cave

Palsabangon Falls

There are two passage ways to get into the fascinating Palsabangon falls, one way is from the Barangay Hall Bagumbungan which is approximately 3.11 kilometer away and can reach the falls within 35-40 minutes of walking. The way from Barangay Bagumbungan is easier to access than the trails from Barangay Ilayang Palsabangon. The distance from the barangay hall of Ilayang Palsabangon to Palsabangon Falls is approximately 3.57 kilometers with approximately 45 mins of trails to walk.

Location: Palsabangon Falls

Palsabangon Rivers

The Palsabangon River is known for continuously and leisurely flowing water from the waterfalls that the residents known as “Palsabangon Falls”. The water that flows from Ilayang Palsabangon River is connected to Ibabang Palsabangon River and mixes into Tayabas bay. It is an extensive river. Some portions of the water are deep and some are shallow. Various groups of rocks that have been worn and rounded by the flowing water are called “river rocks” which are found in the river bed and come in a range of sizes, colours and textures. The fresh water is flowing in the river and due to its clear water, the river rocks can be seen clearly even on the deep part of it. The river is surrounded with lush plants, tall trees and brown earth soil that comes with a loamy texture. Due to the loamy texture of the soil near the area of the river, it causes the river to have muddy colour when the rainy period comes. Also, there’s a hanging bridge in the river that serves as a linking way for the residents of Brgy. Ilayang Palsabangon to the other side of the river. The river is very accessible because the end of the concrete road of the barangay is beside the river.

Location: Palsabangon River

Patayan Island

Patayan Island looks like an inverted liver when viewed from the map of Brgy. Pinagbayanan, Pagbilao, Quezon. It is a twenty six and ninety-eight hundredth (26.98) hectare-island that is abundant with different schools of fish namely, talakitok, baliwis, kanuping etc. From Daungan Port, it will take twenty to thirty minutes before reaching the island with a boat. The bigger and the faster the boat suggests lesser time of travel. Upon reaching the island, a picturesque sandbar can be seen which is popularly called “tulay buhangin” by the locals which is mostly visible at dawn. It is surrounded by the following barangays: Pinagbayanan, Mapagong, Bantigue and Palsabangon. The well-known power plant, Team Energy, can also be seen from the island.

Location: Patayan Island

Rizal Monument

The present Church of St. Catherine of Alexandria was constructed in 1845 under Fr. Victorino Peralija. Together with the convent and belfry, it was completed in 1877 under Fr. Eugenio Gomez. After it was destroyed during liberation from World War II, it was repaired in 1954 under Fr. Vicente Urlanda in 1954.

Location: Rizal Monument

Sibatiya Cave

Sibatiya is located a few meters from the high mountains of Lima-aw in Brgy. Ilayang Polo. The land where the cave is situated is owned by the Marciano family. It only takes about twenty (20) minutes of travel time to reach the cave from its nearest landmark, the Sanggunian ng Brgy. Ilaya Polo building. Sibatiya has two (2) huge entry ways. First is the main entryway that measures about 10 meters in diameter. The second entryway which is considered to be the end passage measures about 6 meters in diameter. With its two huge entryways, a large percentage of light passes through inside making the visuals and features of the cave visible to the eye. Its flooring is covered in goat Capra aegagrus hircus manure, evident that the cave was used to house goats that belonged to the owner of the land. Other faunas that are visible living inside the cave are bats and a particular bird that is called balinsasayaw Collocaliini.

Location: Sibatiya

Tambak River

Tambak river is a 4,112.31 meter long riverbank that connects five barangays of Pagbilao namely, Mayhay, Tambak, Ikirin, Pinagbayanan and Daungan. Among the other rivers in Pagbilao, Tambak River is the most famous and can be easily accessed by locals because it is located in one of the town’s major barangay. Although the river is near the town proper, the rear side counterpart of the poblacion which is Brgy. Mayhay is still surrounded with different forms of greenery such as trees like bamboo, madre cacao, banana and mango. Different types of freshwater faunas are found living in the river. There are “hito” (catfish) siluriformes, tilapia (St. Peter's Fish) Oreochromis niloticus, hipong-tabang (Suahe) Metapanaeus ensis and many others that are very small to classify.

Location: Tambak River
